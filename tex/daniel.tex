\textit{
The Septuagint (LXX) version of Daniel is not the earliest Greek form
  of the book; rather, it is largely a later revision that replaced an
  older Greek translation known as the Old Greek (OG). The OG version
  of Book of Daniel differs noticeably in wording, order, and
  sometimes content, often being freer and more paraphrastic, while
  the LXX (in practice, the revision associated with Theodotion) is
  closer to the Hebrew/Aramaic text and more literal. Because the OG
  text diverged significantly, it was mostly supplanted in Christian
  tradition by the Theodotionic/LXX form, though the OG survives in
  some manuscripts and is important for textual criticism. In this
  edition an AI-generated translation of the Old Greek appears in the
  third column labelled (OG).
