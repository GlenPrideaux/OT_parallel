  \textit{The books of 1 and 2 Samuel and the first chapter and a bit
  of 1 Kings are notoriously problematic in the Masoretic Text (MT) that
  English Bibles are mostly translated from. The Old Greek that forms
  the text for the Septuatint (LXX) fixes some of the MT's errors, but
  has its own problems. There exists a
family of Hebrew and Greek copies that are sometimes called the
``Palestinian'' copies. These copies include the ``Lucianic'' Greek copies and 
Hebrew Dead Sea Scrolls of Samuel, particularly that called 4Q51, also
called 4QSam\textsuperscript{a}. For the more obvious errors or
omissions, many English translations will fill the gap with the
reading from the Palestinian copies, but many remain uncorrected. In
  this edition we present a translation of the Palestinian text
  labelled (P) in a third column beside the World English Bible and
  an English translation of the Septuagint. This third column extends
  from the beginning of 1 Samuel to the second chapter of 1
  Kings. (The (P) text does not stop there, but after that point the
  MT is no longer unreliable.) }
